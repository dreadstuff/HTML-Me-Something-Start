If you're in St Louis - we recommend visiting Hoffman's Two-toed sloth!

Here's little bit about Hoffman's Two-toed sloth:


All two-toed sloths have a number of adaptations related to their upside-down, arboreal lifestyle. They are the only mammals to have hair curves from stomach to back. Also due to their upside-down lifestyle, their face hair grows “up,” which guides water off their faces when it rains. They have about half of the muscle mass of comparable mammals, giving them a light body weight that is useful for living in trees. Their body temperature is exceptionally variable, which enables them to conserve energy.

Did you know? Sloths have evovled to have a slow metabolism, which assist with moving slowly and camouflage in the trees.

How extreme is the life of a sloth?

5 Cool Sloth Facts

  1. Slow Movers, Fast Digesters: Sloths move so slowly that algae grow on their fur, but their unique digestive system can take up to a month to fully digest a single leaf!
  2. Masters of Camouflage: Thanks to the algae on their fur, sloths blend perfectly into the green canopy, making it hard for predators to spot them.
  3. Hanging Out, Literally: Sloths spend about 90% of their lives hanging upside down from tree branches, even sleeping in this position for 15-20 hours a day!
  4. Strong Swimmers: Surprisingly, sloths are excellent swimmers! They use their long arms to efficiently paddle through water, crossing rivers in search of new trees.
  5. A Mysterious Smile: Sloths are known for their seemingly permanent smile, but this is just how their facial structure is formed, not necessarily an indication of their mood.
